Afterglow TV — Layout Concepts

Four screen-level redesigns that move Afterglow away from the TiViMate template into its own visual logic. Each concept solves a specific problem TiViMate's UI doesn't solve. All four assume the Neon Dusk palette but every theme would inherit the same layout — themes change color, layout changes thinking.

Density without clutter

Show more programs at once than TiViMate's classic grid by collapsing dead chrome — no left rail on Guide, no permanent channel slab on Live TV.

Magazine over grid

Default home is curated rails ("On Now", "Tonight at 8", "Up Next on Favorites") — readable like a TV magazine, not a wall of cells.

Floating, not nailed-down

Channel switcher and quick-settings are glass cards floating over the live video — not opaque slabs that hide it. The current channel stays visible while you browse.

Accent stripe is the voice

Every focusable surface — rows, cards, fields, even program blocks — wears the leading accent stripe. Cohesive system, instantly recognizable.

What's different

TiViMate (and most IPTV apps) drop you on Live TV with a slab of channel names. That's a tuner, not a home screen. Afterglow's home treats the EPG as the front door — what's on right now is the hero, and "what's coming up" is the dominant rail.

  • Top tabs, no left rail — gives the magazine its width
  • Hero shows your last-tuned channel with live progress + description
  • Up Next rail on the right is just your favorites' next program — one-keypress jump
  • "Tonight at 22:00" shelf below: the next time-block across all channels, magazine-style
vs TiViMate: they have no "home". Live TV is the start; you press LEFT to see channels; you press MENU to see EPG; the launcher leaves you tuned to whatever was on last. Afterglow's home gives you the shape of the evening before you pick.

What's different

TiViMate's iconic Live-TV-with-side-panel covers ~32% of the picture with an opaque slab any time you want to switch channels. Afterglow's deck is a horizontal glass card row anchored at the bottom — the picture stays visible, your eye doesn't have to re-acquire context.

  • D-pad LEFT/RIGHT scrolls the deck horizontally
  • Focused card grows — 240×108 dp vs 180×92 idle, with a -8 dp lift
  • Glass effect — 75% surface + 20 dp blur — picture bleeds through
  • D-pad UP from the deck → full EPG grid (Concept 3)
  • D-pad DOWN dismisses the deck → picture fully unobstructed
vs TiViMate: they hide the picture with a 320 dp wide opaque slab. Afterglow keeps the picture front and center while you browse, like the iOS Control Center / Apple TV channel switcher.

What's different

Traditional EPG grids are read-only walls of titles. TiViMate's grid is a clean execution of the classic Gemstar format, but you have to leave the grid to learn anything about a program. Afterglow splits the screen: the top half is the grid you'd recognize; the bottom half is a live "story stack" for whatever program is focused.

  • Top half: 3-hour grid, red now-line with glow, focus on Amanpour
  • Bottom half: auto-updates as you move focus — poster, description, cast, length, broadcast features (subs, live), and three primary actions
  • "More like this" rail surfaces related programs across channels and days — discovery built into the EPG
  • Pills use the accent palette — News, Live, Subtitles, etc. — easy parse on a TV
vs TiViMate: grid only. To see a synopsis you press OK which navigates away from the grid. Afterglow's grid is the browse view — you never leave it.

What's different

TiViMate's OSD lives in the bottom third — opaque, full-width, blocks the picture for 6+ seconds. Afterglow's OSD floats: a glass strip anchored to the top with channel & program info, a small quick-action rail on the right edge (subs / audio / aspect / speed / record), and a discoverable key-hints strip at the bottom.

  • Top glass strip — 75% surface + 20 dp blur + accent border — auto-dismisses 3 s after channel change
  • Right edge rail — five quick-actions stacked vertically — D-pad RIGHT to enter the rail
  • Bottom key hints — discoverable controls, not a feature; visible only when OSD is open
  • Never covers the picture — the corner regions are blurred glass, the center stays clear
vs TiViMate: their OSD steals the bottom third of the screen and there's no quick-action shortcut — you have to open the full settings panel to toggle subtitles. Afterglow's rail is one D-pad press away from a focused channel.
